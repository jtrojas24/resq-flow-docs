`resq-flow` works best when we are explicit about what belongs in the graph and what does not.

These principles exist so we do not guess our way into a noisy or misleading flow view.

## The core ideas

- real queue and worker boundaries stay first-class by default
- meaningful decisions and processes can also be first-class
- important does not automatically mean first-class
- the graph should stay sparse while drill-down stays rich
- canvas detail and logs should agree on what counts as useful default signal
- the spec should come before implementation

## Why this matters

Without a clear mental model, one of two bad things happens:

- every important write, handoff, or helper step becomes a node
- or the graph stops being truthful about the actual runtime boundaries

`resq-flow` is trying to avoid both.
