If you are new to observability tooling, here is the short version.

## What observability means

Observability is the ability to understand what a system is doing from the signals it emits while it runs.

The goal is to answer questions like:

- what happened
- where did it happen
- how long did it take
- what failed
- what happened right before or after

## What telemetry means

Telemetry is the raw data a system emits so we can observe it later.

In our world, the main telemetry types are:

- `logs`
  human-readable or structured event records
- `traces`
  linked execution spans that show timing and parent-child relationships
- `metrics`
  numeric measurements like counters, rates, and latency summaries

## What OTLP means

`OTLP` is the transport format used by OpenTelemetry tools to send telemetry around.

For us, the important idea is simple:

- `resq-agent` emits OTLP
- `Vector` receives it
- the stack routes it to the places that need it

## The stack pieces in plain English

### resq-agent

`resq-agent` is a producer. It emits the traces and logs that describe real queue, worker, and process activity.

### Vector

`Vector` is the collector and router.

It receives telemetry once and fans it out to multiple downstream systems.

### Victoria

`VictoriaLogs`, `VictoriaTraces`, and `VictoriaMetrics` are the durable storage and query surfaces in the local stack.

If you want long-lived storage and flexible querying, this is the primary source of truth.

### resq-flow relay

The Rust relay is the flow-aware consumer.

It receives traces and logs, normalizes them into a shared `FlowEvent` model, applies exact flow matching, and streams them to the UI.

### resq-flow UI

The Vite UI is the presentation layer.

It turns those normalized flow events into:

- graph views
- log views
- run and journey views
- inspectors and flow detail

## Why resq-flow exists on top of this stack

Logs, traces, and metrics are necessary, but they are not automatically shaped around a product flow.

`resq-flow` exists to add that missing layer:

- execution boundaries become easier to see
- meaningful steps stay visible
- low-value noise can stay in deeper views
- the same flow model can drive both live and historical understanding

Telemetry is the raw material.

`resq-flow` is one of the tools that turns that raw material into something easier to reason about.
